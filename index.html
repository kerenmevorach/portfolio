  • Googlie

  • Product Design

  • 2023

Problem

The high cost of crafting supplies prevents people from exploring their craft, and finding new passions.

Solution

Googlie. A crafting marketplace app that allows users to borrow and lend crafting supplies. The app aims to inspire crafters and provide them with the opportunity to explore new passions without committing to purchasing new equipment.

Desk & Observational Research

I started off the project by conducting desk and observational research. I researched where crafters get their crafting supplies, and inspiration for craft projects. I also observed how people make decisions when shopping for crafting supplies in stores.

Meta Insights & How Might We

I then conducted interviews with 6 crafters, with crafting experience ranging from beginner to expert. These interviews helped me understand crafters' pain points, and led to 4 key meta insights and how might we statements.

Low Fidelity Prototypes & Testing

The meta insights and how might we statements led to 4 low-fidelity prototypes, which I then tested with 5 users.

    Prototypes & Takeaways:

  • Prototype #1:
    WeWork for crafters—Allows users to rent spaces for crafting projects. Users said that the crafting rental spaces must offer something unique. Otherwise, users would craft at home.
  • Prototype #2:
    DoorDash for crafting supplies—A crafting supply delivery service. Users said they are more likely to use Amazon, or a crafting supply website like Michael's instead.
  • Prototype #3:
    Rent the Runway for crafting supplies—A crafting supply rental service. Users wanted to ensure this was a legitimate and trustworthy company they coudl rent from.
  • Prototype #4:
    Facebook Marketplace for crafting supplies—A crafting supply marketplace that allows users to borrow, lend, buy and sell crafting supplies. This resonated with users the most, but they wanted to have the opportunity to explore and get inspired.

Mid Fidelity Prototypes & Testing

I then created 2 mid fidelity prototypes based on the crafting marketplace concept. The first prototype allowed users to explore, the interface is more familiar to other marketplace apps, and it allowed users to be indecisive. The second prototype provided a guided experience, the interface is more unfamiliar to other marketplace apps, and it forced users to make decisions each step of the way.

    User Testing Takeaways:

  • Users want a balance between a guided and exploratory experience
  • Users liked seeing visuals throughout the process
  • Users want to narrow their search after selecting a category
  • Users prefer to choose between borrowing or buying later on in the process

Branding

Googlie is all about exploring, whether it’s through discovering new hobbies, searching for materials, or finding new passions. This idea of exploration and searching is represented through the brand’s logo, a pair of googlie eyes.

High-Fidelity Prototype

The final prototype maps out the journey as a borrower searching for crafting materials, and as a lender making a product listing.

    User Testing Takeaways:

  • Users want a balance between a guided and exploratory experience

    Borrowing an Item:

  • Users can find products on the explore tab, where a random set of products are shown to provide inspiration
  • Users can search for a specific product if they know exactly what they are looking for
  • Users can also browse by category if they have a specific type of project in mind

Listing a New Item

  • On the lend page, users can view the items they are currently lending, and other items they have listed
  • Users can add a new product listing where they fill out the name, description and category of the item. The user will then upload photos, set a pickup address, and choose a price for their product
  • When choosing a price, users are asked to provide the monetary value of the product. From there, the app will automatically calculate suggested daily, weekly and monthly rates. Users can then decide which rates they would like to charge

  • Eggsploration

  • Book Design, Photography

  • 2022

Eggs! They’re often viewed as mundane objects. But oddly enough, they can be near and dear to our hearts. I’ve had many experiences and memories associated with eggs throughout my life. I noticed that without even realizing it, eggs have been my inspiration for quite some time.

This project explores all things eggs. I studied the egg’s form through a wide variety of methods including stories, photography, form studies, and lettering. I expressed my design process through the lens of an everyday object—the egg. Check out the type tool to use the custom egg inspired lettering 🥚
