  • Googlie

  • Research, UX/UI Design

  • 2023

I've always been a crafty gal! Recently, I wanted to start a new project that required me to purchase a sewing machine. I wasn't sure if it would be worth the investment, and whether I would get enough use out of the machine. I decided not to purchase it, and I sadly abandoned my project idea. Googlie was inspired by my own, and many others' struggle to explore their craft.

Problem:
The high cost of crafting supplies prevents people from exploring their craft, and finding new passions.

Solution:
Googlie, a crafting marketplace app that allows users to borrow and lend crafting supplies. The app aims to inspire crafters and provide them with the opportunity to explore new passions without committing to purchasing new equipment.

Desk & Observational Research:
I started off the project by conducting desk and observational research. I researched where crafters get their crafting supplies, and inspiration for craft projects. I also observed how people make decisions when shopping for crafting supplies.

Interviews:
I then conducted interviews with 6 crafters, with crafting experience ranging from beginner to expert.

Meta Insights & How Might We Statements
These interviews helped me understand crafters' pain points, and led to 4 key meta insights and how might we statements.

Low Fidelity Prototypes & Testing:

Low-Fi Prototype #1:
Since crafters don’t always have enough space to craft at home, they now have the option to rent spaces to craft. They can rent an area in a communal work space intended for crafters.

    User Testing Takeaways:

  • These spaces must offer something unique. Otherwise, users would craft at home
  • Overall, it would be hard for users to justify paying for a space for a hobby

Low-Fi Prototype #2:
Crafters can now order crafting supplies on demand! Similar to a food delivery app, crafters can add items to their cart based on a particular crafting store, and have their items delivered the same day.

    User Testing Takeaways:

  • Users are more likely to use Google, Amazon, or a crafting store website instead
  • Users would only use this if they needed something specific in a pinch
  • This solution eliminates all exploratory aspects of searching for crafting supplies

Low-Fi Prototype #3:
Users now have the option to rent crafting supplies so they don’t have to commit to buying. Users can find supplies they want to rent, and select how long they need the supplies for.

    User Testing Takeaways:

  • Users would like to see more details about the terms of their rental
  • Overall, this prototype really resonated with users

Low-Fi Prototype #4:
Crafters can buy and sell, or borrow and lend supplies to each other. Users can search for specific materias, or browse by different crafting categories. Once they find what they are looking for, users can directly message the seller/lender of the item.

    User Testing Takeaways:

  • It’s important for users to see more product and seller details
  • Users would like to see a discover/explore page

Mid Fidelity Prototypes & Testing:
I then created 2 mid fidelity prototypes based on the crafting marketplace concept.

Mid-Fi Prototype #1:

  • The interface is visually similar to other marketplace apps
  • It allows users to explore
  • It asks users to decide if they wanted to borrow or buy something towards the end of their search

Mid-Fi Prototype #2:

  • The interface is visually different from other marketplace apps
  • It provides users with a very guided experience, where they curate their search for materials every step of the way
  • It asks users to decide if they wanted to borrow or buy something at the very beginning of their search

Mid-Fi Testing Key Takeaways:

  • Users want a balance between a guided and exploratory experience
  • Users liked seeing visuals throughout the process
  • Users want to narrow their search after selecting a category
  • Users prefer to choose between borrowing or buying later on in the process

Scoping:
I used the MSCW framework to help me determine my MVP features. I decided to focus my first prototype on the borrowing and lending process, since it felt more unique, and would likely be the most beneficial for users. I plan to incorporate the buying and selling process in a future prototype. I then created a system map to help me determine the overlap flow of the app.

Branding:
Googlie is all about exploring, whether it’s through discovering new hobbies, searching for materials, or finding new passions. This idea of exploration and searching is represented through the brand’s logo, which is a pair of googlie eyes.

High-Fidelity Prototype:
The final prototype maps out the journey as a borrower searching for crafting materials, and as a lender making a product listing.

Borrowing an Item:

  • Users can find products on the explore tab, where a random set of products are shown to provide inspiration
  • Users can search for a specific product if they know exactly what they are looking for
  • Users can also browse by category if they have a specific type of project in mind

List a New Item:

  • On the lend page, users can view the items they are currently lending, and other items they have listed
  • Users can add a new product listing where they fill out the name, description and category of the item. The user will then upload photos, set a pickup address, and choose a price for their product
  • When choosing a price, users are asked to provide the monetary value of the product. From there, the app will automatically calculate suggested daily, weekly and monthly rates. Users can then decide which rates they would like to charge

Reflection:

  • I really learned about the importance of user testing. I conducted user testing with my low, mid, and high fidelity prototypes. This allowed me to make changes and adjustments to my prototypes along the way, and really understand what was resonating with users
  • I learned that this is an interative process! There is no such thing as finishing something and moving on right away. I always returned to previous steps to continue updating and refining
  • I learned how valuable sketching can be, even at a later stage in the process. I found myself returning to sketching while making high-fidelity prototypes

Next Steps:

  • Incorporating buying and selling in addition to borrowing and lending
  • Adding a calendar view so borrowers can see when a product is available
  • More testing of my high-fidelity prototype 👀
